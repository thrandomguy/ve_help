ECONOMIC DISASTER

The Venezuela of 20 years ago was filled with smiling and happy people all around the country, the minimum monthly salary was around $600 and with that you could buy anything you needed to sustain yourself and your family, providing clothing, computers, shoes, toys, vacations, houses, cars, we could afford anything.

Today however minimum wage is around $4.7 as of the moment, and this goes lower and lower every week because of the huge inflation this country is going through, even when minimum wage is increased in our currency (vef) every few months. It’s right now sitting at 450’000VEF monthly, and each USD equals to 95500VEF right now (Back in the year 2000 one USD was equal to 0.570VEF.

You can check USD and VEF relation by date at this website https://dolartoday.com/indicadores/ just scroll down to the first chart, click it and download the document.

For perspective here is a list of prices for a few products as of today (07/12/2017):

and the list goes on, rice, pastas, toothpaste, veggies, fruits…

With a minimum monthly salary (that’s about 35 hours of work every week for a whole month) being 450’000VEF, people that live on minimum salary are only eating once a day, and they are not even eating fully nutritional meals. Those who also have a family that they need to sustain are going through worse times as they have to stretch those 450’000VEF to feed 2 or 3 people instead of just one.

Most people are not eating meat more than only once a week, some people go a whole day without eating. A lot of people are sorting through the trash bins behind markets and restaurants scavenging for foot that they can take back home to their families. Kids are going to school without breakfast and they return home over 7 hours later after class without eating anything for the whole day because a lot of public schools are not serving lunches any more due to lack of funding.

Public government funded places for the elderly where they are supposed to spend their last few years of life without worries after having worked hard during their youth are not being funded anymore, so now they are receiving donations from other people in order to be able to provide basic needs for the elderly who live there most of which don’t have families to get them out of there or help them in any way. They are not even receiving the medicine that they need to keep the pain of being old away.

Regular people are constantly asking for help to get the medicine that they need, it is really heartbreaking and unbelievable but people are dying of preventable diseases because they can’t afford medicine, even small diseases. And big ones like cancer are a death sentence no matter how early it’s detected as equipment to treat it is almost non existent and where it exists the treatment is extremely expensive.

As if all of that wasn’t enough, there’s now an epidemic of diphtheria, a disease for which vaccines have existed for several years and people are dying because they can’t get their vaccines.

This Is just a summary of everything that people are going through, there are a lot more tragedies, like public hospitals running with no funding at all and having to reuse syringes on the same patients two or three times, not giving proper meals, not having proper equipment to the point where patients are being laid on the ground as even the beds are broken down, a huge number of new borns are dying each month because of equipment malfunctions, power outages, and other reasons, a lot of women giving birth are dying on the beds of the hospital.

Giving birth in a public hospital is really risky due to the bad conditions public hospitals are operating on, but a lot of people can’t pay to give birth in a proper clinic or private hospital.

And there are a lot more issues and we’re trying to help as much as we can.

HOW WE HELP

We started by just serving meals to as many people as we could reach on our own, the number of meals we were providing was around 50 meals each day. That’s what we could afford to do at a rate that could keep us going for at least a few weeks.

Then as more donations started coming we could grow to help people in other ways, back when we were just providing meals a lot of people had asked us if we could also help them with medicine that they needed, some others that couldn’t walk had asked us if we could get them wheelchairs, those who are homeless had asked us if we could provide clean clothes, toilet paper, soaps, sheets and pillows...

So we started to further help people by providing medicine, clean clothes, shoes, soaps, toilet papers and so far 3 wheelchairs that we could purchase second hand at a good price but that were in perfect condition. We also started to provide food to a few churches that also serve meals for free everyday as well as providing a weekly supply of food for an elderly institution in our town which hosts 80 people.

There’s a lot of room to go further and help a lot more people, in times when even working hard won’t allow you to eat more than once a day, everyone needs help. The streets are full of people begging for anyone to help them, to give them food because they are starving, to give them medicine that they need, kids asking you to help them with some food because his family at home haven’t eating for the whole day.

Parents having to watch their kids lose their health and not being able to do anything at all no matter how hard they try.
